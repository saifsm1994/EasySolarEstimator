Solar Panel Used

For the purposes of this model we have used the Canadian Solar CS6U - 330M Panel

Power: 330W

Dimensions: 1960 x 992 x 40 mm (77.2 x 39.1 x 1.57 in)

Efficiency: 16.97%

Degredation rate: 0.5% per year

Cost per watt of capacity:

Low: $1.54 per Watt of Solar Capacity (Cost of a DIY 1200W kit / 1200W)

 High: $3.22 per Watt of Solar Capacity (Average Cost in Q1 2017 USA)

Formula

We have used a formula E = A * r * H * PR

Where,

E equals Energy Output from the panel in kWh

A equals the panel's surface area

r equals the efficiency of the panel, which also equals max output/area

H equals annual Global Horizontal Irradience

PR equals a performance ratio used to account for general losses in the solar PV system. We have set this to a default value of 80 percent(this value generally ranges from 0.5-0.9)

Assumptions

1. That the panels are kept clear of debris and operational at all times

2. That the temperature range and other extraneous factors and potential sources of efficiency loss can be acounted for by our PR of 80%

3. We assume that the panel is operating under standard test conditions, and conditions are within manufacturer guidlines. Wind or temperature deviations outside standard testing conditions may affect panel output.

4. That the panel is perfectly horizontal. (Tilting the panel may improve performance based on geolocation)

5. The system being modeled is an on-grid system and as such we do not account for battery or power storage costs.
